City Slicker

Fast-paced movement FPS focused on speed and flow

5 Weeks Solo C# Unity

Game Overview

City Slicker is a fast-paced movement-based first-person shooter developed completely solo in Unity using C#. The game was created over five weeks with the goal of experimenting with responsive high-speed traversal mechanics.

All gameplay systems, mechanics and assets were created from scratch without external libraries or packages.

Game & Development

City Slicker focuses on building momentum and speed while jumping between buildings and eliminating turrets along the way.

The core goal was to make movement feel fluid, responsive and fun, encouraging players to maintain speed while managing risk.

During development I encountered several issues with Unity's Animator system. I eventually replaced it entirely with code-driven animation triggers, which gave me more control and reliability.

This project helped deepen my understanding of object-oriented programming concepts such as polymorphism, encapsulation and inheritance.

What I Learned

  • Improved understanding of OOP principles
  • Learned strengths and limitations of Unity's animation system
  • Practiced modular and reusable code architecture
  • Gained valuable solo development experience
